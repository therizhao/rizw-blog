# One-Liner

**"Alerts you when your website looks broken to users, even if your API says everything's fine."**

---

# Validation Steps (4 Hours Total)

## **Hour 1: Community Research**

**Goal: Find 10+ people with this exact pain**

- Post in 3 Reddit communities (r/webdev, r/nextjs, r/reactjs):
    - "How do you know if your users are seeing a broken UI in production?"
- Join 2 Discord servers (Indie Hackers, Frontend Developers)
    - Ask: "Ever had your site break visually but uptime monitor said it's fine?"
- Check Twitter for recent complaints:
    - Search: "website broken but API working"
    - Search: "frontend error production"
- **Success metric:** 10+ people respond with "yes, this happened to me"

## **Hour 2: Landing Page**

**Goal: Can explain it in 30 seconds**

Build on Carrd/HTML:

- Headline: "Know when your frontend breaks before your customers do"
- Subheadline: "Screenshot monitoring that alerts you when users see broken UI"
- 3 bullet points:
    - ✅ Takes screenshots every 5 minutes
    - ✅ Detects visual changes automatically
    - ✅ Alerts via Slack/email when something breaks
- Pricing shown: "$19/month"
- Email capture form
- **Success metric:** Clear enough that you'd sign up

## **Hour 3: Traffic Test**

**Goal: 30-50 email signups**

Post in these places (spend 10 min each):

1. **Reddit r/webdev**: "I'm building a tool that screenshots your site and alerts when UI breaks. Would you use this?"
2. **Twitter**: "Your API returns 200 OK but users see a blank page. How do you catch this before they complain? (I'm building something for this)"
3. **Indie Hackers**: "Validating idea: Screenshot monitoring for frontend devs"
4. **Dev.to**: Quick article "The blind spot in uptime monitoring"
5. **HN Show**: "Landing page for screenshot monitoring tool"
6. **Product Hunt Ship**: Pre-launch page

Link to landing page in all posts.

**Success metric:** 30+ email signups in 24 hours

## **Hour 4: Customer Interviews**

**Goal: 3+ people commit to paying**

Email the 10 most engaged signups:

vbnet

Copy code

`Subject: Quick question about frontend monitoring  Hi [name],  You signed up for [tool]. Quick question:  What was the last time your frontend broke in production  but your monitoring didn't catch it?  Would you pay $19/month to prevent that from happening again?  - rizw`

Jump on 15-min calls with 5 of them.

Ask:

1. "Tell me about the last frontend incident you had"
2. "How did you find out about it?"
3. "What would you pay to catch it automatically?"
4. "If I built this, would you be my first customer?"

**Success metric:** 3+ say "yes, I'd pay for this"
